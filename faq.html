FAQ

What is FATHOM?

A channel for honest signal. People share what they actually think, in their own words, on their own device. The AI structures and synthesizes it and delivers the picture to the people who decide, with identity protected where it must be. It surfaces what surveys and climate polls miss.

Why anonymity, instead of just asking people directly?

The most valuable signal is exactly what people will not say to your face. Rank, consequence, and plain politeness keep it unspoken. Where a response is anonymous, that personal risk is removed and the truth can travel, while the insight is preserved.

How is anonymity protected?

Raw responses never travel upstream. FATHOM holds the responses and delivers only synthesis: patterns, themes, and flags, not a record of who said what. The protection is built into how the system works, not promised in a policy.

Can a response be traced back to whoever gave it?

Where a response is anonymous, only the synthesis reaches a decision-maker, and identifying detail, including a person's own writing style, is removed first. Before submitting, the person sees exactly what will leave their hands and whether it is anonymous or attributed.

How does the AI handle harsh or emotional input?

A hard, even hostile, response is reduced to the valid point inside it and rephrased so it can be heard, with the cruelty and the identifying voice removed.

Is anything sold or used to train AI?

No. Responses are kept only to produce the report the organization receives. They are never sold and never used to train models.

How many responses does a report need?

At least three. Below that, there is neither reliable signal nor real protection for the people responding.

What can FATHOM be used for?

Anywhere the cost of unspoken truth is high: assessing people fairly, reading the health of a team, catching a problem early, understanding why people leave. The same engine serves each, configured to the question being asked.
